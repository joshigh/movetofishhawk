Why FishHawk Ranch Is a Parent's Dream: The Schools Say It All

In a region where finding one A-rated public school in your zone is considered a win, FishHawk sits in a position most Tampa Bay communities can't match — a complete pipeline of A-rated public schools from Pre-K through 12th grade, most of them either inside the community or within a couple miles of it.

If you've ever talked to someone who moved to FishHawk Ranch, it usually doesn't take long before the conversation turns to the schools. In a region where finding one A-rated public school in your zone is considered a win, FishHawk sits in a position most Tampa Bay communities can't match — a complete pipeline of A-rated public schools from Pre-K through 12th grade, most of them either inside the community or within a couple miles of it.

The Elementary Schools: Where It All Starts

FishHawk is served by two elementary schools within its community borders, and both carry serious academic reputations.

Bevis Elementary School, located at 5720 Osprey Ridge Drive, sits right inside Phase 2 of FishHawk Ranch and is one of the most decorated public elementary schools in the state. U.S. News ranks Bevis 26th among all elementary schools in Florida, and 91% of students are proficient in math and 86% in reading — well above both Hillsborough County and state averages. The school has earned Blue Ribbon status along with Reading, ESE, and Math Teacher of the Year honors, and has been consistently recognized as a Top Performer on Florida's state assessments. Families in the Osprey Park and surrounding villages can walk their kids to school from a number of streets in the area.

FishHawk Creek Elementary, located at 16815 Dorman Rd off the Dorman Road corridor on the eastern side of the community, serves that portion of FishHawk and carries its own strong reputation as an A-rated school with a GreatSchools score of 8 out of 10.

Then there's Stowers Elementary, the newest addition, sitting inside FishHawk Ranch West at 13915 Barrington Stowers Drive — a genuine neighborhood school that's walkable, community-oriented, and already earning strong marks.

The Middle Schools: Building on a Strong Foundation

Randall Middle School sits at 16510 FishHawk Blvd, right along the main boulevard on the north side of the road and just next door to Newsome High School. It serves a large portion of the FishHawk community and has consistently held A and high B ratings from the state.

Barrington Middle School, at 5925 Village Center Drive, is tucked inside FishHawk Ranch West alongside Stowers Elementary — giving families in that section of the community a school path from kindergarten through 8th grade without ever really leaving the neighborhood.

Newsome High School

Everything in FishHawk feeds into Joe E. Newsome High School, located at 16550 FishHawk Blvd — right next door to Randall Middle, making the transition between schools a short walk down the same campus area.

Newsome is one of the top high schools in Florida by most measures. U.S. News ranks it 45th in the state, and 72% of students participate in Advanced Placement coursework. In the 2024-25 school year, 81% of 10th-grade students scored proficient or better in English Language Arts, compared to 57% for Hillsborough County and 58% for the state.

The school's marching band has performed in the Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade, and Newsome has received recognition for having an Elite Athletic Program from Hillsborough County. Students also have access to the Bright Futures Scholarship Program, which can cover 75% to 100% of public college tuition at Florida colleges and universities — a meaningful long-term benefit for families thinking about the cost of higher education.

The Bigger Picture

FishHawk is one of the very few zones in the Tampa Bay area where every level — elementary, middle, and high school — carries an A-rating, with most schools located either inside the community or within two and a half miles of it.

For families relocating to the Tampa area and weighing their options, that kind of consistency across every grade level is genuinely hard to find. When people describe FishHawk as a community built for families, the school system is a big part of what they're pointing to.
